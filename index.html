Endnotes, Glossary & Key Terms

The complete back-matter apparatus, online

The printed book does not carry its endnotes, glossary, and key-terms section. That material runs to roughly 133 pages, and every endnote ends in a clickable source link. On the printed page those links are dead ink. Here they are live, and they are the point.

The argument in this book rests on what the cited authorities actually hold. The endnotes are where a reader checks that work — each citation follows Chicago style and closes with a working link to the original source, so any claim can be traced to its document in a single click. Whether you want to verify the case law, the cost figures, the patent lineages, or the historical record, this is where it lives.

The links are not the only advantage. Online, the whole section is searchable: a quick find (Ctrl-F, or Command-F on a Mac) jumps straight to the phrase, case name, or glossary term you have in mind, instead of paging through 133 pages to locate it.

What this section contains

  • Endnotes — the numbered citations for every chapter, each ending in a clickable source link.
  • Endnote Commentary — expanded notes that go beyond a bare citation where a point needs it.
  • Appendix A: General Notes on the Supreme Court Cases — a plain-language note on each Supreme Court holding the argument relies on.
  • Appendix B: Note on AI-Assisted Authorship — a disclosure of how the manuscript was researched and assembled.
  • Glossary — definitions of the technical and legal terms used throughout the book.
  • Key Terms — a quick-reference list of the recurring terms of art and how this book uses them.
